Colombo Swimming Club
Your Bill Was Sent.
Just Not To You.
The club's own records show that 62% of active members — 4,432 people — have incomplete contact information. Bills sent to incorrect addresses go undelivered. The member never knows. The club knows the data is wrong. No routine process exists to fix it before bills go out.
When a bill goes unpaid — whether received or not — a surcharge of 10% per month is applied. At that rate, a missed bill nearly doubles in 7 months and triples within a year. The member remains unaware. The debt compounds — and keeps compounding.
Once the debt crosses a threshold, membership is terminated. The deposit — held by the club as security against bills — is consumed by the compounded debt. The member loses both their membership and their money. For a bill they never received. A good member is lost to the club — permanently.
The questions the GC must answer
×
Is a 10% monthly surcharge rate fair? And does a rate this punishing give the club any incentive to maintain accurate contact data in the first place?
×
Were members with incorrect contact data proactively notified before surcharges were applied?
×
How many terminations resulted from undelivered bills — and how many deposits were retained against surcharge debts?
×
Why is there no monthly mid-cycle review of contact data accuracy before bills are issued? This is basic administration — not a complex fix.
A routine mid-month check of contact data before bills are issued would prevent this entirely. No such process exists. Good members are being lost — and their deposits consumed — through a failure of basic administration that this GC has chosen not to fix.
No Check. No Warning. No Accountability.
