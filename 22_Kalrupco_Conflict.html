Colombo Swimming Club — Post 22
The Firm Recording Your Minutes.
The President's Own Company Is Their Client.
CSC engaged Kalrupco Management Services (Private) Limited to attend General Committee meetings, make audio recordings, and prepare the official minutes — a role that places them at the centre of the Club's most sensitive deliberations, with access to everything discussed by the committee that governs your Club.
Kalrupco's own published website lists Dev Engineering (Private) Limited as a client — for company secretarial and accounting services — with Mr. Himanthe Dewendre named as the contact. The Registrar of Companies' records confirm it: Kalrupco Management Services (Private) Limited is the registered Company Secretary of Dev Engineering (Pvt) Ltd — a company of which the President, Himanthe Danapriya Dewendre, is a Director.
The firm CSC pays to record its GC meetings also serves the President's own private company — with the President himself listed as their contact for that engagement. This is a direct personal connection between the President and a service provider appointed by the Club he leads. The Constitution requires that any such conflict be declared and the member concerned withdraw from all related decisions.

What the Constitution requires
Rule 9(g)(iii): no Committee member shall allow personal interest to take precedence over the needs of the Club.

Rule 9(g)(iv): if there is a conflict of interest in connection with any proposal, the member must inform the Committee and must not be present when the matter is discussed.
