Colombo Swimming Club
Trustees:
Your Protection May Not Hold
Article 18 of the Constitution protects Trustees and Committee members from personal liability — but only where they have acted in good faith, in the best interests of the Club, and in strict accordance with the Constitution.
That protection falls away where the Constitution has not been followed.
Trustees who knowingly authorise a payment that bypasses mandatory procurement or applies Club funds to purposes not authorised by the Constitution may find Article 18 offers them no shelter.
The Trustees would do well to consider this carefully — before approving any payment.
Governance Matters. Act Accordingly.
