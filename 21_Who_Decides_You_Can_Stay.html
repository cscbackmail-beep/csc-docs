Colombo Swimming Club — Post 21
487 Members Already Gone.
2,100 More at Risk. No Explanation Given.
Just before the 2025 database migration, CSC had 5,090 active members. By April 2026 — per the VP/Finance Member's own communication to members dated 20 May 2026 — that figure had fallen to 4,603. That is 487 active members gone. No reconciliation report. No audit. No communication to members explaining who was removed or why.
The same communication now proposes a cap on active membership of approximately 2,500 — to be put to the next General Meeting. There are currently 4,603 active members. That means a further 2,100 members could be removed. More than one in three of you reading this. The communication discloses no methodology, no criteria, and no constitutional basis for how the cap would be applied.
The Constitution gives the General Committee no express authority to reduce the membership to a fixed number. Whatever is put to a vote at a General Meeting must come with a full, published methodology — who stays, who goes, on what criteria, and who decides. Members cannot be asked to vote on a bare number with no explanation.

487 members were removed in silence. Now over 2,100 more face the same fate. Members deserve answers before any vote is held.
