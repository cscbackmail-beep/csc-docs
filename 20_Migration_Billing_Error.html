Colombo Swimming Club — Post 20
The Migration Corrupted Your Records.
93 Families Were Billed for Deceased Spouses.
The 2025 database migration reversed bereavement records that had been correctly maintained for years. Members whose spouses had passed away — and who had not been charged in prior years — were billed again in 2025. The migration wiped out corrections members had already gone through the process of making.
When raised with the club, they confirmed 93 members were affected. That figure came from the club itself — it is not an estimate. This is not a rounding error or a minor data issue. It is a systemic failure in how the migration was executed and verified.
How many of the 93 noticed? Members whose companies settle bills on their behalf, or who pay without scrutinising the detail, may never have known. The club has made no proactive disclosure to the membership about the scale of this error or what remediation was taken.

A migration that bills families for deceased spouses — and makes no proactive disclosure — is not a technical hiccup. It is a governance failure.
