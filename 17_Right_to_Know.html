New Members Are Joining Your Club.
You Have a Right to Know Who.
CSC is a private members' club. The Constitution requires that new applicants be subject to a ballot of the full General Committee — seven votes needed for election, two adverse votes sufficient to exclude. That process only works if members know who is being considered. The constitutional safeguard exists for a reason.
The full list of applicants called for interview — their proposers, seconders, and GC supporters — is not being circulated to members. Members cannot raise concerns about applicants they do not know exist. The constitutional safeguard is being rendered meaningless.
The waiting list bypassed. Juvenile conversions blocked. Contact data uncorrected. New applicants screened from member scrutiny. This is not a series of administrative oversights. It is a pattern — and the membership of this club is being reshaped while members are deliberately kept from seeing how.
What members should demand
×
Full disclosure of all membership applications currently before the GC — names, proposers, seconders, and GC supporters.
×
A transparent waiting list published to members — showing who applied, when, and where they stand in the queue.
×
A clear, documented process for juvenile conversions — with timelines and criteria published to members.
×
Independent oversight of the membership process going forward — by a group of senior qualified members, not the GC alone.
A private members' club derives its character from its members. That character cannot be protected if members are kept in the dark about who is joining.
No Disclosure. No Scrutiny. No Accountability.
