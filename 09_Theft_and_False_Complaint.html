Colombo Swimming Club
A Theft. A False Complaint.
A Pattern of Misconduct?
What actually happened
1
A Club employee stole a 20-litre oil container, loading it onto a contractor's lorry. The employee admitted the theft and was suspended pending inquiry.
2
Instead of simply dealing with an employee theft, a police complaint was lodged against two elected GC members — A. Rajenthiran and Nadeeka Abeysekera — alleging they chose the contractor without approval and removed CCTV cameras.
3
The Vice President and General Secretary attended Kollupitiya Police Station, gave their statements, and fully cooperated with police.
Their response — allegations denied in full
Contractor appointed through proper procedure
No CCTV cameras were removed
All allegations denied
Full cooperation given to police
Part of a pattern?
This is not an isolated incident. Members have witnessed: a disputed nominations committee process, the unfair dismissal of a long-serving employee, refusal to convene a requisitioned SGM, disputes over GC minutes — and now a police complaint over a routine employee theft.
Targeted? Intimidation? Malicious?
The facts are before the members. Members can judge for themselves.
