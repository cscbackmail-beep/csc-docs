The GC Cannot
Override the Constitution
Some may argue that because the General Committee approved the engagement of these lawyers, the matter is settled. It is not.
The authority of the General Committee is derived entirely from the Constitution and must be exercised subject to it. The GC does not possess authority to waive, override or retrospectively validate non-compliance with mandatory procurement requirements.
Only the Constitution itself or the Membership in General Meeting can authorise such a departure. The GC acting alone cannot.
A GC resolution does not cure a constitutional breach. Only the Membership can do that.
The Constitution is Supreme.
