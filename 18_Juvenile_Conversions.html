Colombo Swimming Club
Juvenile Members Have a Right to Convert.
Hundreds Are Still Waiting.
A juvenile member who turns 21 — or 23 if in recognised full-time education — is entitled to convert to full Primary membership at 25% of the entrance fee. This is not a privilege. It is a right earned by years of membership. After that age, the right is lost — permanently.
The club's established process required the Member Services Executive to contact both the Primary member and the juvenile proactively when the juvenile turned 20 — giving a full year to complete the conversion before the right expired. That process no longer appears to be happening. The club's own October 2025 database recorded 594 active juvenile members over 21 — of whom 489 are over 23, meaning they have already passed the maximum conversion age and lost their right entirely. Some of these figures may include conversions not yet updated in the database — but even allowing for that, the scale of failure is undeniable. Members are losing a right the club had an obligation to protect.
A member who has belonged to this club since childhood — swum in these pools, grown up in this community — can lose their right to full membership simply because the club failed to act. No resignation. No wrongdoing. A failure that falls entirely on the member, for a process the club itself was responsible for.
What should happen now
×
All eligible juvenile conversions should be processed immediately as a priority — before any new external applications are considered.
×
Of the 594 over-21 juveniles in the October 2025 database, 489 are already over 23 — past the maximum conversion age. Those who aged out due to the club's own failure should be granted a special dispensation — conversion at the 25% rate regardless of current age.
×
Juvenile members pushed into inactive or terminated status during this period should be offered reinstatement on the same basis — at a reduced entrance fee, not as new applicants.
×
The proactive outreach process at age 20 must be reinstated immediately and maintained as a standing obligation of the Member Services Executive.
These are not new members asking for a favour. They are existing members asking for what they were always entitled to.
No Process. No Urgency. No Accountability.
