The Juvenile Collapse. Active juvenile members fell from 1,419 to 815 — a drop of 604 in six months. In the same period, only 28 juvenile conversions were processed against 670 eligible. Members who have belonged to this club since childhood are being lost — not converted, not retained. No rationale has been given.

Proposed Membership Cap. The President's 20th May 2026 communication proposes a cap of approximately 2,500 members — against a current active membership of 4,603. That is a proposed reduction of over 2,100 active members. No methodology for how this cap would be applied has been disclosed to members.

The Questions the GC Must Answer

What was the documented rationale for the membership data cleanup? What criteria were applied and who decided them?
Who authorised and supervised the cleanup? What methodology was used? Were removed members notified?
Why were members not informed and why has no report been presented at a General Meeting?
How will the proposed cap of 2,500 be applied — and who decides who stays and who goes?
A cleanup of this scale requires independent oversight by a group of senior qualified members — not unilateral action by the General Committee alone.
