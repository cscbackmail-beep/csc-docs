CSC — Membership Data

What the membership database showed before and after the 2025 migration

1. The Headline Drop

A snapshot of the membership database taken just prior to the 2025 migration recorded 5,090 active members. The President's communication of 20th May 2026 reported 4,603 active members as at 16th April 2026 — a reduction of 487 active members. No reconciliation report, no audit, and no explanation has been provided to the Membership.

A migration that results in the loss of 487 active members requires a reconciliation — a line-by-line check of who was lost, why, and whether they should be restored. No such reconciliation has been disclosed to members.
2. The Juvenile Collapse

Juvenile members who turn 21 — or 23 if in full-time education — have a constitutional right to convert to full Primary membership at 25% of the entrance fee. The club's established process required proactive outreach to eligible juveniles at age 20. That process has stopped. The migration appears to have accelerated the loss.

Of the 594 active juveniles over 21 in the pre-migration snapshot, 489 are already over 23 — past the maximum conversion age. They have lost their right permanently. Not through any fault of their own. Because the club stopped doing what it was supposed to do.
3. The Proposed Cap

The President's 20th May communication proposes that the General Committee put to the next General Meeting a cap on membership of approximately 2,500 — against a post-migration active membership of 4,603. That is a proposed reduction of over 2,100 further active members.
